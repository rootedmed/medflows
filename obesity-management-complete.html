Comprehensive Obesity Management Tool

This tool provides information on FDA-approved medications for obesity management.

Medication 1: Orlistat (Alli, Xenical)

Mechanism: Reduces the absorption of fat.

Dosage: Usually 120 mg taking with each meal containing fat.

Side Effects: Gastrointestinal issues, fatty stools.

Medication 2: Phentermine-topiramate (Qsymia)

Mechanism: Appetite suppressant and anticonvulsant.

Dosage: Start with 3.75 mg/23 mg, adjusted as needed.

Side Effects: Tingling, dizziness, constipation.

Medication 3: Liraglutide (Saxenda)

Mechanism: Mimics GLP-1 to reduce appetite.

Dosage: Start at 0.6 mg, escalate to 3 mg.

Side Effects: Nausea, low blood sugar.

Medication 4: Semaglutide (Wegovy)

Mechanism: GLP-1 receptor agonist.

Dosage: Start at 0.25 mg, increase to 2.4 mg.

Side Effects: Gastrointestinal issues, risk of pancreatitis.
